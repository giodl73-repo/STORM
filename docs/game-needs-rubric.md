# STORM Game-Needs Scoring Rubric

Score scenario candidates and fixture records 0-4 on what downstream games need
from STORM. This rubric does not score generic meteorological sophistication.
A candidate is game-ready for prototype use when every axis is at least 3 and no
boundary-discipline axis scores below 4.

| Axis | 0 | 2 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hazard legibility | Weather is ambience only. | Hazard type is named but pressure is vague. | Player-facing hazard pressure is clear enough for downstream systems to act on. |
| Warning/timing | Event simply happens. | Season or warning exists. | Lead time, seasonality, signal confidence, and timing uncertainty are playable. |
| Exposure specificity | Everything is generally affected. | One exposed asset or group is named. | Exposed asset, population, place class, or activity is specific and bounded. |
| Preparedness tradeoff | No preparation choice exists. | A preparation is listed. | Preparation changes exposure while costing labor, material, route access, water, fuel, legitimacy, trust, or time. |
| Recovery window | Consequence ends instantly or indefinitely. | Recovery action is named. | Earliest action, likely duration, and risk of overlapping hazards are explicit. |
| Cross-system handoff | STORM absorbs all outcomes. | One sibling repo is mentioned. | Each consequence has a named owner and STORM stops at the handoff. |
| Playable pressure | Scenario is descriptive only. | It suggests a decision. | It creates a concrete downstream planning, triage, timing, or prioritization pressure without defining mechanics. |
| Evidence/certainty | Claims lack source or confidence posture. | Evidence or confidence is implied. | Evidence refs, warning confidence, false-alarm possibility, and validation hooks can be represented. |
| Boundary discipline | STORM defines maps, prices, ecology, legitimacy, identity, narrative, or mechanics. | Boundaries are partially named. | PORTO/CERES/FAUNA/FLORA/RITE/BANISH/CANON/LUCIA boundaries are explicit and respected. |

## Use

Use this rubric after the foundation rubric when deciding which scenario
candidates should become fixtures. Prefer candidates with strong warning/timing,
exposure specificity, and recovery-window pressure, because those gaps recur in
`docs\scenario-expansion.md`.
